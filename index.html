Peshna Cave

The Peshna Cave in North Macedonia is famous for its huge entrance, often compared to scenes from The Lord of the Rings. With towering rocks, stalactites, and hidden passages, it’s one of the largest and most stunning caves in the Balkans. Nearby, the ruins of a medieval fortress add to its ancient charm.

Itsukushima Shrine

The Itsukushima Shrine on Miyajima Island, Japan, is famous for its floating torii gate that rises from the sea at high tide. Built in the 12th century and dedicated to the Shinto sea goddess, it’s a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Japan’s most iconic landmarks.

Barcelona Cathedral

The Barcelona Cathedral, officially known as the Cathedral of the Holy Cross and Saint Eulalia, stands proudly in the heart of the city’s Gothic Quarter. Built between the 13th and 15th centuries, this magnificent structure is a masterpiece of Catalan Gothic architecture, Although construction finished in the 15th century, the stunning Neo-Gothic façade was added in the 19th century, completing the cathedral’s timeless beauty. The cathedral remains one of the city’s most enchanting and historic landmarks.
